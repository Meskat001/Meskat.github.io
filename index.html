Example: Maxwell's Equations

Maxwell's equations are a set of fundamental equations that form the basis of classical electromagnetism. In their differential form, they are:

Gauss's Law for Electricity: $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\varepsilon_0}$

Gauss's Law for Magnetism: $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0$

Faraday's Law of Induction: $\nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t}$

Amp√®re-Maxwell's Law: $\nabla \times \mathbf{B} = \mu_0 \left( \mathbf{J} + \varepsilon_0 \frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t} \right)$
